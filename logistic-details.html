Driving & Care Tips for Safari

On Safari Roads
  • Always engage 4x4 low range before rough terrain or sand.
  • Reduce tyre pressure to 18‑20 PSI on soft sand (we provide compressor).
  • Never drive through floodwater without checking depth.
  • Respect park speed limits (max 40 km/h inside reserves).
Daily Checks
  • Check oil & coolant level each morning (dipstick provided).
  • Inspect tyre pressure and condition before long drives.
  • Keep fridge box running when engine is on – avoid battery drain.
  • Report any warning lights immediately via WhatsApp.
What to Avoid
  • ❌ Driving with pop‑up roof open on tarmac roads.
  • ❌ Using low‑quality diesel (only major stations recommended).
  • ❌ Parking in deep grass (fire hazard).
  • ❌ Crossing private land without permission.
Emergency Kit Provided
  • 1x spare wheel (full size)
  • Heavy‑duty jack & lug wrench
  • Air compressor & tyre repair kit
  • First aid kit & 2 warning triangles
  • 24/7 roadside assistance (phone number in glovebox)
